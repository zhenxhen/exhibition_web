InterPlay presents work by emerging artists, designers, and researchers currently enrolled at UAL Creative Computing Institute's postgraduate courses. Rather than treating technology as a neutral tool, this exhibition examines the conversation present between artistic expression and technology.

Exhibitors identify with a diverse pool of professional backgrounds: music and audio, software engineering, computer vision, robotics, HCI, UX/UI, interaction design, game development, graphic design, fine arts, philosophy, spatial design, and AI.

Witness and interact with the next generation's responses to our contemporary world, addressing themes such as the anthropocene, IoT, hidden worlds, vulnerability, ontology, memory, classification, community, and migration. This collection exemplifies collaboration, not only between global creatives originating across five continents, but also among circuitry, scripting, bodies, and play.

This exhibition invites you to participate in the InterPlay ecosystem–to play within the latent space of humanity and technology. Whether through technologies such as TouchDesigner or Unity, p5.js or physical computing, these works guide our understanding of reality with technology as material. InterPlay invites visitors to explore the mystique located at this intersection.
